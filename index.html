About

I recently completed my Undergraduate Degree at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) in Computer Science. Currently, I am a Research Programmer for Andrew McCallum's Information Extraction and Synthesis Laboratory (IESL) at the the University of Massachusetts at Amherst where I am working on REXA (scientific literature search engine) and FACTORIE (probabilistic programming framework). Before that, I was a Research Programmer for Dan Roth's Cognitive Computation Group (CCG) at UIUC where my research was primarily focused on Textual Entailment.

Factorie

FACTORIE is a toolkit for deployable probabilistic modeling, implemented as a software library in Scala. Providing users with a succinct framework for creating abritrary factor graphs with tied parameters, estimating parameters, and performing inference.

Textual Entailment Annotation and Visualization

An annotation interface for textual entailment. The current version is only tested on Mozilla Firefox 3.0+. I have an IE7+ fix in a new release coming soon as well as some new features, such as edge selection. I am interested in ways I can improve it; feel free to send me comments or hack around with it.

Fun

Lowrider

I often find myself in a situation where people do not believe me when I tell them about my old cars. So, I found a few videos sitting on an old external hard drive to prove my point. Also, I received the Senior Superlative of "Best Car of the Senior Class" at American Heritage School in 2004 (maybe I should scan the yearbook page). I'm not sure which car got me the award. I wish I documented my adventures in customizing these two cars better, but unfortunately this is the extent of the documentation I have.

My first car (video) - a gorgeous 1984 Chrysler Fifth Avenue with red velvet interior, purchased for a mere $900. I installed the hydraulics myself, a non-trivial task -- the fifth ave has torsion bar suspension and short A-Arms.
My Second car (video) - "The Big Bad Baby Blue Buick" the hydraulics are on the back of this one, you can't tell in this video, but the car was scrapping the ground a bit.
The videos don't show any of the great stuff on the interior, such as train air-horn, fog machine, the 1400 Watts of stereo power, television and Playstation 2, various neon lights, or the custom dashboard. It definitely does not show the cracked chassis that I had to welded back together or the water pump, alternator, starter motor, and carburetor my dad and I fixed. Working on these cars was an invaluable learning experience and a load of fun.
